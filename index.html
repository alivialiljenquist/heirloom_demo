Who We Are

A Group Built on Craft,
Community, and Operational Clarity

The Heirloom Group is a Utah-based restaurant group and hospitality collective founded on the belief that extraordinary dining experiences are built from the inside out — through disciplined systems, exceptional sourcing, and a relentless focus on the guest.

Founded in Provo, Utah by Kevin Santiago and Colton Soelberg, our portfolio spans eight distinct concepts — from approachable neighborhood cafes to full-service dining rooms — each engineered to reflect its community while operating at the highest standard of efficiency and craft.

We do not build restaurants by formula. We build them by conviction. Every concept begins with a question: what does this community need, and what does it deserve? The answer shapes everything from menu architecture and sourcing partnerships to floor plan flow and staff systems.

Our Approach
◆

Producer-First Sourcing

We allow the inherent quality of ingredients from local farmers and regional producers to drive our menus, not the other way around.

◆

Systems That Scale

From recipe standardization to operational scheduling, we build repeatable, flexible systems that maintain quality across every concept.

◆

Community Accountability

We operate in the communities we serve — accountable to neighbors, families, and the local economy our restaurants support.

What We Do

Five Pillars of Hospitality Excellence

Our capabilities span the full lifecycle of a restaurant concept — from initial vision through daily operations and long-term brand growth.

01

Restaurant Concept Development

We develop restaurant concepts from the ground up — defining positioning, cuisine direction, atmosphere, target guest profile, and financial feasibility before a single dollar is committed to construction or staffing.

  • Concept ideation & market positioning
  • Feasibility analysis
  • Brand voice & identity framework
  • Business plan development
02

Culinary Strategy & Menu Design

Our menus are built to perform — financially, operationally, and experientially. We partner with producers, engineer for kitchen efficiency, and design each dish to reinforce the concept's core identity.

  • Menu architecture & engineering
  • Recipe development & standardization
  • Producer sourcing partnerships
  • Seasonal & pricing strategy
03

Operations & Systems

Profitable restaurants run on disciplined systems. We design and implement the operational infrastructure — from staffing models and cost controls to training protocols — that allows quality to be consistent and scalable.

  • Cost structure & margin optimization
  • Staffing models & scheduling
  • Training systems & SOPs
  • Technology & POS integration
04

Guest Experience Design

We engineer the full guest journey — from reservation flow and welcome sequence to table pacing, menu presentation, and service recovery — so that every touchpoint reinforces the emotional intent of the concept.

  • Service flow mapping
  • Hospitality standards development
  • Atmosphere & sensory design
  • Feedback loop systems
05

Brand & Hospitality Consulting

Beyond our own portfolio, we apply our operational and brand-building methodology to partner businesses — advising on concept pivots, turnarounds, growth strategy, and market entry for restaurateurs across the region.

  • Brand repositioning & turnaround
  • Multi-unit growth strategy
  • Hospitality culture development
  • Partnership & acquisition consulting

Project Spotlights

Eight Concepts.
One Standard of Excellence.

Each restaurant in our portfolio represents a distinct community need, culinary identity, and operational challenge — built and operated under the same disciplined framework.

Full-Service Dining • Farm-to-Table

Communal

Heirloom's flagship full-service concept in downtown Provo. Communal operates on a producer-first sourcing model with a menu that rotates to reflect seasonal availability. The concept anchors the group's culinary identity — demonstrating that ingredient-driven cooking and operational discipline are not in conflict.

Operational Focus: Farm-to-table sourcing model, seasonal menu engineering, full-service hospitality standards

Fine Dining • Contemporary American

Bistro Provenance

An elevated contemporary dining concept that brings refined technique and considered hospitality to Provo's dining landscape — a proof of concept that Heirloom's infrastructure can support fine-dining execution.

Operational Focus: Fine-dining service standards, premium sourcing, wine program development

Casual Dining • Artisan Pizza

Pizzeria 712

A wood-fired artisan pizza concept built around local sourcing and simple, direct flavors. Pizzeria 712 applies the producer-first philosophy of Communal in a more casual format — proving the model scales across dining categories.

Operational Focus: Artisan production systems, local ingredient sourcing, casual-dining efficiency

Cultural Dining • Native American Fusion

Black Sheep Cafe

One of the most distinctive concepts in Utah's restaurant landscape. Black Sheep Cafe brings contemporary Native American Southwestern cuisine to a broad audience — a concept built on cultural respect and culinary curiosity.

Operational Focus: Cultural cuisine positioning, community storytelling, diverse menu architecture

Neighborhood Cafe • All-Day Dining

Station 22 Cafe

An approachable, community-forward cafe concept built for high-volume, consistent execution. Station 22 demonstrates Heirloom's capacity to deliver quality at accessible price points without compromising the warmth of the guest experience.

Operational Focus: High-volume throughput, accessible price architecture, neighborhood hospitality model

Barbecue • Southern American

Five Star BBQ

A Southern barbecue concept built for consistency at scale. Five Star BBQ demonstrates Heirloom's ability to execute low-and-slow production methods within a systematic operational framework — maintaining quality across high-volume service periods.

Operational Focus: Production scheduling, consistent low-and-slow execution, value-forward pricing

Fast Casual • Premium Burgers

CHOM

A fast-casual burger concept that reflects the group's range across formats and price points. CHOM applies the same quality-sourcing discipline as the group's full-service concepts within the speed and efficiency required of fast-casual operations.

Operational Focus: Fast-casual throughput, premium ingredient sourcing, rapid-service systems

Market & Cafe • Midway, UT

Heirloom Common & Market

Heirloom's first concept outside Provo, expanding into the Midway and Heber Valley communities. A hybrid cafe and curated market model that deepens community integration and extends the brand's producer-sourcing mission.

Operational Focus: Multi-format concept, market expansion strategy, hybrid retail-dining model
